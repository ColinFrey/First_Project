Your Mamma's Kitchen

Grand's

Lemon Bars

headnote

"These lemon squares are a staple in my kitchen whenever I want something bright and simple. I first started making them for family birthdays because they’re a lot easier to pull together than a layer cake, but they always get more compliments. I really love how the buttery crust balances out the tartness of the lemon, and I've found that using extra zest gives them a much better kick than just using juice alone." —Mr. Grand

Ingredients:

  • Butter: 1/2 cup (1stick), melted; the base for the shortbread crust.
  • Sugar: 1/4 cup. Sweetens both layers and helps the lemon curd set.
  • Flour: 1 cup. Provides structure to solidify the crust.
  • Vanilla Extract: 1 tsp. The "secret ingredient" for a flavorful crust.
  • Salt: 1/4 tsp. Balances the sweetness of the shortbread.
  • Eggs: 3 large, room tempature. The primary structural agent for the filling.
  • Lemon Juice: 1/2 cup (approx. 2-3 large lemons). Freshly squeezed is best.

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven: Arrange a rack in the middle and heat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Make the crust: Combine the melted butter, sugar, vanilla extract, salt, and flour until a dough forms. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of the prepared pan and bake until light golden. Glass pans work best!
  3. Make the filling: In a medium bowl, whisk the eggs and sugar until well combined. Stir in the freshly squeezed lemon juice and a small amount of flour to help the curd set.
  4. Bake: Pour the lemon filling over the hot, pre-baked crust. Return to the oven and bake for 18–20 minutes, or until the filling is set with only a slight wiggle in the center.
  5. Cool: Allow the bars to cool on a wire rack at room temperature for 1 hour, then refrigerate for at least 3 hours (or overnight) to ensure the curd is fully firm.
  6. Serve: Carefully remove bars from the pan. Slice into squares and, if desired, finish with a dusting of powdered sugar before serving.
